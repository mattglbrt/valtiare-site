Manira is a layered world. Above, there are forests and mountains and long
roads. Below, there are great caverns of shimmering stone where whole peoples
live their lives without ever seeing the sky. For most of its long history,
the two halves barely knew each other.

Then the earth fractured.

No single account of the Fracturing agrees with another. What is certain is
that something ripped open the skin of Manira in a long jagged seam, and the
crevasses it left behind were not simply geological. Sunlight poured down
into caverns that had never known it. Voices from below surfaced in forests
that had never heard them. Creatures that had lived on one side of the stone
for a thousand generations began to find their way to the other.

Some call the Fracturing a catastrophe. Some call it a blessing. A few — a
strange few — believe the world broke itself open on purpose.
