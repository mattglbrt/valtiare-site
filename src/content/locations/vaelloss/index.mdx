Vaelloss turns slow beneath a sun that gives more memory than heat. Its oceans have
gone shallow, its forests petrified into strange glass-like groves, and its cities
cluster around the last warm places — volcanic seams, thermal springs, the ruins of
furnaces that no one remembers building.

The Vaellossians are a soft-spoken people who measure wealth in kept flame. Fire is
inherited, never bought. To let a hearth die is to end a lineage.

Travelers come here to listen. The world has a way of telling its own eulogies.
