No one is sure whether the Orchard was ever alive. Some Vaellossian scholars claim the
glass trees grew from seeds of cooled starlight; others that the forest is a single
organism wearing the shape of many. Either way, the branches hold tiny motes of warm
light inside them, as if the orchard kept something from the old, bright years and
refuses to let it go.

Walking through the Orchard at night, a visitor's own breath becomes visible in the
faint glow from the canopy. Sound moves strangely here. Words spoken near a trunk
often return, softened, many hours later.

Pilgrims from the surrounding settlements leave small offerings at the base of the
tallest tree — the one the locals call **the Keeper**. It is said to remember every
prayer it has ever been told, and to tell them back in order, one at a time, to anyone
patient enough to listen.
