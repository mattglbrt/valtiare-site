The Age of Ash begins when the last of the great stars gutter and go. It is not an
age of sudden catastrophe but of slow unravelling — of heat retreating from stone, of
candles guarded as inheritance, of kingdoms measured in how long their hearths can hold.

In the Ashen years, travel between worlds becomes a kind of pilgrimage. The sky-roads
that once burned bright are now faint, cinder-thin things that only the devoted can
follow. What is remembered is remembered carefully; what is lost stays lost.

This is the era in which most of the Valtiare stories are told.
