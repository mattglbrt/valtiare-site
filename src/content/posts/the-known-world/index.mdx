This is the world entire.

It has no single agreed-upon name. The scholars of Tanion call it *the
Wheel*. The pilgrims of Algard call it *Onebreath*. Most people, most of
the time, do not call it anything at all. When you live inside a thing,
you do not need a name for the thing — only for the places within it.

What the map shows is twelve countries, arranged around a central sea and
a long spine of mountains running north to south through the western
landmass. Three of them — the eastern three — have already had their
quiet begun.

## The East, and the Fracturing

At the hinge of the eastern coast lies **Manira**, the country the
Fracturing broke. The travelers who pass through what remains of it now
call the place the **Fractured Isles**, a broken archipelago of stone
rafts drifting in the air and held together by a warm light no one has
named. South of it, across a short border, lies **Ruhar**, a country that
answered the Fracturing by growing — old fields and cities swallowed over
a single long year into one vast and tangled wilderness. North of Manira
lies **Emuin**, a country that answered the Fracturing by going hollow —
fields pale, beasts gaunt, children quieter than they should be.

These three are neighbors. What happened to Manira reached each of them,
and each of them answered in its own way. No one yet agrees on what
happened to Manira in the first place.

## The Rest, Still Mostly Names

The other nine countries are, for now, names on a map and not much else.

**Thean** and **Kallhamar** share the great western continent, with its
long mountain chain that has swallowed more expeditions than any other
feature in the known world. **Algard** sits in the northern highlands.
**Tanion** and **Myr** lie at the center, where the continental weather
gathers and the trade routes thread together. **The Fist**, further north
and west, is a hook of dark land reaching into a cold sea. **Glaice** is
a small, lonely country of ice and silence. **Esnor** is a sunburned
southern island. And **the Spire** — the Spire is the Spire, and the
people who live there prefer it that way.

Each of these countries has its own reckoning of the Fracturing, and its
own quiet theory about what caused it. No two of them agree. Some of
them may not be wrong.

## The Map Itself

The map above is the first complete survey of the known world that has
been drawn since the Fracturing. Where older maps show Manira whole, this
one shows it shattered. Where older maps show Ruhar as rolling country,
this one shows a green unbroken by any road. Where older maps show
Emuin's cities, this one shows them faintly, as if afraid to commit to
them.

The cartographer is not named.

The world has not been renamed, and probably will not be. The places
within it continue to be what they have always been: themselves, and the
stories growing inside them.
