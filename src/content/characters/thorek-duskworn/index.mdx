Once a priest of a temple long forgotten, Thorek knelt at the bedside of a
dying child and begged the heavens for mercy. The heavens gave nothing. He
left the temple that night with the child's doll bound at his throat — a
symbol of the faith he had lost and, though he did not yet know it, the seed
of a new one.

For decades he wandered as an apostate. He learned herbs and poultices by
necessity, took up sword and shield by necessity, and kept walking because he
could think of nothing else to do. A healer without blessing. A priest
without faith.

In the forests of Ruhar, over the body of a dying Svirfneblin, his hands
burned with light for the first time in his long exile. It did not come from
any god. It came from him.

He travels still, tending the wounded he finds along the road. He no longer
prays. He believes the divinity he once begged for was never the gods' alone
— that mortals have always carried it, and that some of them, one day,
remember.
