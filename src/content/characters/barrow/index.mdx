Barrow belonged to Garrin before anything else in Garrin's new life did.
Where he came from — whether he was rescued, found, or simply chose to
follow — has never been explained and has not, apparently, ever needed
to be.

He walks at Garrin's heel, ears flicking at every sound, nose always
searching. He greets Triste with a thump of his tail and then goes back
to watching the trees. On the road he sleeps lightly. In camp he is the
first to hear anything that should not be there.

Garrin talks to Barrow more than he talks to anyone else. This seems to
suit them both.
