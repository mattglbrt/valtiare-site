Garrin keeps his past in silence, the way he keeps his weapons oiled — a
matter of habit and long practice. Once a soldier in campaigns he does not
speak of, now a hunter and a reluctant bodyguard, he moves with the economy
of someone who learned young that motion costs.

Somewhere along the shared road with Triste, his loyalty grew into something
deeper. He has not spoken of it, and she has never asked. His closeness to
her shows in the quiet glances and in the way they walk in step without
thinking.

His fuller story is still being told.
