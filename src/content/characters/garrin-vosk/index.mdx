Garrin Vosk moves through the forest with the quiet certainty of someone
who has walked away from the life he once understood. He came from an
ordered world where every dawn brought a command and every night brought
another name to remember. Now he lives in the spaces between those
memories, drifting from place to place with only his hound Barrow for
company.

The weight of his chainmail rests on his shoulders like an old habit —
familiar, grounding, and impossible to fully set aside. He wears it not
out of duty, but because he no longer knows what he is without it.

Triste finds him whenever she wishes, appearing in his camp or on the
trail with the same ease that moonlight finds an opening in the trees.
When she calls, he answers.
