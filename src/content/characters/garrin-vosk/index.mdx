Garrin Vosk came from an ordered life — one where every dawn brought a
command and every night brought another name to remember. He does not speak
of what that life cost him. What he has now is the weight of old chainmail
he cannot quite bring himself to set aside, a worn road that never repeats
the same way twice, and a hound named Barrow who follows at his heel and,
some days, is the only proof he is still connected to the world at all.

He drifts. The forest seems content to let him drift — except that Triste
finds him whenever she wishes, appearing in his camp or on his trail the
way moonlight finds an opening in the trees. When she calls, he answers.
It is the closest he has come to meaning in years.
