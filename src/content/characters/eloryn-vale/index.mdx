Eloryn Vale presents himself as a wandering scholar — soft-spoken, endlessly
curious about ruins and fallen civilizations, the kind of man who travels
with too many books and not enough survival skills. His robes are worn at
the seams. His notes are extensive. His voice is calm enough to suggest a
life spent in dusty libraries.

This version of himself is a lie he has cultivated with care.

Beneath the gentle manners sits a mind consumed by hunger. Eloryn gathers
knowledge not to understand, but to use. Every relic, every glyph, every
fragment of arcane theory is another step in the hidden work of his life.
His staff, which he calls a tool of study, is in truth an anchor for a
ritual he has been constructing for years. He intends to shed mortality
entirely — to take a place among the greater forces of creation, no matter
what it costs him or anyone else.

He studies the people he travels with the way he studies arcane diagrams.
They do not yet know.
